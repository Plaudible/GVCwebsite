The History

       In 1910, the noodles we call Ramen today first appeared in Japan, when Chinese cooks at a restaurant called Rairaiken created a signature dish that had broth and Chinese noodles, that were more yellow and stretchy than traditional Japanese noodles, since their dough was kneaded with kansui, a sodium-carbonate-infused alkaline mineral water, also known as Lye water. (Click image to learn more about lye). At this time, these noodles were called shina soba. Shina is a word that means China, and Soba means buckwheat noodles. In other words, to eat shina soba in those years was to basically eat China itself. As China represented the empire's biggest prize, a bowl of shina soba represented world domination to the Japanese.

      After Japan lost the war in World War 2, the word shina was considered extremely racist to the Chinese. So shina soba was soon renamed chuka soba, chuka meaning Chinese-style. But in 1958, Nissin Foods introduced the first-ever packaged instant version of the noodles. As its broth was chicken-flavored, the product was called Chikin Ramen.From there, the industry grew. Instant noodles spread worldwide and became a big hit in the US and many areas in Asia, as it still is today.
